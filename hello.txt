1. Use a Proper Maskable Icon
Make sure your icon files (especially those with "purpose": "maskable") are true PNGs with a transparent background and no black border or shadow.
Use maskable.app to generate a maskable icon that fills the safe area and looks good on all devices.
How to update:

Replace /icon-192x192.png and /icon-512x512.png with new maskable PNGs.
Ensure the icon fills the canvas and has no extra transparent padding.
2. Remove Black Border from Icon
Open your icon in an image editor (Figma, Photoshop, GIMP, etc.).
Remove any black outline or shadow.
Export as PNG with a transparent or white background.
3. Double-Check Manifest Icon Paths
Make sure the icons referenced in your manifest actually match the updated, borderless, maskable icons.
Clear your browser cache or uninstall/reinstall the PWA to see changes.
4. Provide Custom Splash Screens (Advanced)
For full control, you can provide platform-specific splash images using the pwa-asset-generator:

Run the tool to generate splash screens for all devices and add them to your manifest.
This ensures your splash looks exactly as you want on every device.
5. Test on Multiple Devices/Browsers
Sometimes, old icons are cached. Try on a different device or clear the app data/cache.
Uninstall and reinstall the PWA from your home screen.
6. Fallback: Use a Centered Splash Div (Web Only)
If you want a custom splash while the app loads (not the OS splash), use a splash div as described earlier, then hide it after your app loads.
